Overview

The Energy Intensity Index (EII) Predictor is a machine learning model designed to forecast EII values, aiding in industrial energy optimization. The model leverages an ensemble of feed-forward neural networks and a Random Forest to improve accuracy and reliability across multiple datasets.

Project Highlights

  • Developed a hybrid ML ensemble combining Neural Networks and Random Forest.
  • Implemented 5-fold cross-validation for robust model evaluation.
  • Utilized Median Absolute Deviation (MAD) normalization to enhance model performance.
  • Automated energy cost assessments through real-time prediction and analytics.

Technical Details

The model architecture consists of 10 feed-forward neural networks trained per dataset and averaged for ensemble robustness. A Random Forest model complements the neural networks, with predictions combined through a MSE-based weighting mechanism. The training process incorporates feature scaling, outlier-resistant normalization, and automated model selection.

Outcome

The EII Predictor demonstrated improved forecasting accuracy over traditional regression methods. It enables proactive decision-making in energy-intensive industries, optimizing energy costs and improving sustainability metrics.
