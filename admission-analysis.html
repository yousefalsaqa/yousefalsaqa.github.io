Admission Analysis Dashboard

A data analysis tool that processed 10 years of admission data, simulated policy changes, and contributed to the university raising Commerce admission thresholds.

Role Sole Developer
Timeline Sep 2024 — Dec 2024
Stack Python, Tkinter, Pandas
Impact Policy change

The Problem

Working in Queen's admission office, I noticed something: we had years of data but no good way to explore it. Every question — "How many international students from UAE applied last year?" or "What's the acceptance rate trend for Commerce?" — required manual spreadsheet work.

Worse, when policy discussions came up, decisions were often made on intuition rather than data. "What if we raised the Commerce cutoff by 2%?" Nobody could answer that quickly.

The Solution

I built a desktop application that connects to our admission database and lets anyone explore the data through an intuitive interface.

Core Features

  • Multi-dimensional filtering: Filter by year, program, country, school, citizenship status, admission status — any combination.
  • Instant visualization: Charts update in real-time as you adjust filters. See trends over time, distributions by category, comparisons between groups.
  • Policy simulator: "What-if" analysis. Adjust admission thresholds and see how many students would have been affected in previous years.
  • Export everything: Any view can be exported to Excel or PDF for reports and presentations.

The moment it mattered: During a policy meeting about Commerce program capacity, I ran a simulation showing that raising the threshold by 2% would have reduced admissions by ~15% over the past 5 years. The data was in front of decision-makers within minutes, not days.

Performance Optimization

With 10 years of data (100K+ records), naive filtering was too slow. I implemented:

  • Indexed SQLite queries instead of Pandas filtering for initial loads
  • Lazy loading for charts — only render visible data
  • Cached aggregations for common query patterns

Result: sub-second response for most queries.

Policy Simulator Logic

The simulator doesn't just apply cutoffs retroactively. It accounts for:

  • Grade inflation trends over time
  • Different grading scales by country/province
  • Conditional vs. final offers

Impact

10 Years of data analyzed
100K+ Application records
3% Commerce threshold increase
<1s Query response time

The dashboard is now used regularly by the admission team for reporting and analysis. The policy simulation feature has been used in multiple program reviews.

Most significantly: the Commerce program raised its admission threshold based on analysis done with this tool. Data-driven decisions instead of guesswork.

What I Learned

  • Build for the question, not the data. Users don't care about your database schema. They have questions they need answered quickly.
  • Simulations need trust. The policy simulator was only useful because I could explain exactly how it worked and what assumptions it made.
  • Desktop apps aren't dead. For internal tools with sensitive data, a local application is often simpler and more secure than a web app.
