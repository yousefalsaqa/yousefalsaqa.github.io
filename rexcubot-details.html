Tronassic Park: Team Rexcubot

Advanced robotic design and implementation for precision and adaptability.

Rexcubot was designed for the Tronassic Park competition to autonomously navigate and complete precision-based tasks. The competition demanded innovation in design, electronics, and control, focusing on object retrieval and autonomous navigation.

Rexcubot in its final assembly, ready for competition.

Scoop Mechanism

The scoop mechanism was designed using a parallel linkage system to ensure uniform motion throughout its range. This approach provided stability and consistent force distribution, crucial for retrieving scattered objects efficiently.

The lightweight aluminum arms of the linkage reduced the inertial load on the servo motor, optimizing energy consumption while maintaining structural integrity. Testing ensured the scoop could retrieve multiple objects in a single motion without mechanical failure.

The parallel linkage-based scoop mechanism, engineered for precision and durability.

Claw Assembly

The claw assembly was designed for tasks requiring high precision, such as rescuing the Ken doll from a pit. The gripper's actuation was controlled via a high-torque servo, ensuring smooth operation under variable load conditions.

The gripper’s geometry was optimized to handle a wide range of object shapes and sizes. Stress analysis ensured the assembly could withstand repeated use without deformation or loss of grip strength.

Precision gripper mechanism for reliable object manipulation.

Control Systems

Rexcubot featured a hybrid control system, combining autonomous navigation with manual gamepad control. The IR sensors facilitated precise line-following capabilities, while the ultrasonic sensors provided obstacle detection and avoidance.

The software architecture was developed in C++, incorporating state machines for task transitions. A UDP-based communication protocol enabled seamless real-time control via a Python gamepad interface.
